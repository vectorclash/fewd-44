Welcome to the Phonometrics Lab

My name is Aaron Ezra Sterczewski, also known as Vectorclash and welcome to this experiment. I'm excited for you to experience what I've created!

This project has been something I've wanted to build for quite some time now. I had been experimenting with Three JS for a few years now. I've only scratched the surface of what it's capable of but it is at the core of everything here. Phonometrics is an interactive sound visualization experiment. One of my primary intents for it will be to use it at various events for music visualization. My goal is to delploy it at Burning Man in the near future, projecting it on the interior of a geodesic dome during a dance party so that it may dance along to the music.

For the visuals I wanted everything to be as colorful as possible! This is of course an ongoing theme in most of my work, as you would expect. For assistance in manipulating color I am using tinycolor which I have found to be invaluable in my work. There is so much that it can do. I could not imagine moving forward without.

The last framework that I rely on heavily here is of course GSAP. Tweenmax along with Three JS make up the core of Phonometrics. I can't state enough how grateful I am that such a thing exists. With just one line of code you can animate an entire webpage.

Instructions

General settings

  1. Background color will adjust the color of the main webgl container. When the gradient sphere is active that background color will not be visible.
  2. Ambient light intensity increases the brightness of the ambient light. This is very important for the icosahedron module due to it being wireframe. There is a directional spotlight also so if the ambient light is turned all the way down that light will be the only one currently lighting the scene.
  3. Fog can be turned on or off for the scene. Activating the fog will allow you to choose its color. Turning it on will also deactivate the gradient sphere as it would just be obscured by the fog.

Chaotic Cubes

  1. Cube number is fairly stratight forward. Simply increase or decrese to add or remove cubes. Removal is instantaneous but adding is done slowly depending on movement speed.
  2. Cube rotation is a toggle between random cube rotation and having their rotation all being aligned. Default is off.
  3. Cube container scale is simple. Increase or decrease the size of the overall container of all cubes.
  4. Cube movement speed is a bit odd. Lower numbers increase the speed of movement. Another important note, The movement speed determines how quickly changes happen. If the movement speed was set to its slowest, then making a change to rotation or speed again won't take effect until a cube has finished that movement.

Icosahedrons

  1. Icosahedron number is fairly stratight forward. Simply increase or decrese to add or remove wireframe icosahedrons. Removal is instantaneous but adding is done slowly per frame
  2. Icosahedron color offset is the amount the color is offset from the base. Adjusting this will change the color spectrum shift of all icosahedrons
  3. Icosahedron container scale is simple. Increase or decrease the size of the overall container of all icosahedrons.

Toroids

  1. Torus number is fairly stratight forward. Simply increase or decrese to add or remove toroids.
  2. Torus sides will adjust the number of sides each toroid shape has. Default is 6 for a hexagonal toroid shape.
  3. Torus thickness adjusts the overall thickness of all toroids.
  4. Torus container scale increases or decreases the size of the overall container of all toroids.

Dodecahedron Flow

  1. Dodecahedron number is fairly stratight forward. Simply increase or decrese to add or remove dodecahedrons. Simply increase or decrese to add or remove dodecahedrons. Removal is instantaneous but adding is done slowly per frame
  2. Dodecahedron x size adjusts the outer limit along the x axis.
  3. Dodecahedron y size adjusts the outer limit along the y axis.
  4. Dodecahedron z size adjusts the outer limit along the z axis.
  5. Dodecahedron speed changes the rotation speed of all dodecahedrons. Moving the slider to the left will decrease the speed and to the right will make everything spin faster.

Gradient Sphere

  1. Gradient colors will allow you to pick three colors for the gradient texture. As you choose colors they are added to the sphere
  2. Voronoi pattern creates a faux voronoi effect by interlacing low polygon spheres. This adds up to 4 together as you change colors.

Nebula Sphere

  1. This creates a textured transparent sphere. You may choose from a small selection of space scene textures. When fog is turned on this sphere is colored by the fog.
